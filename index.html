DSCOVR Satellite

The DSCOVR (Deep Space Climate Observatory) satellite, launched in 2015, is a joint mission by NASA, NOAA, and the U.S. Air Force. It is primarily designed to monitor and provide real-time data on solar wind, solar storms, and space weather, helping scientists better understand and predict solar phenomena that can impact Earth's technology and infrastructure.
